World War Two

Above is the iconic photo taken at the Battle of Iwo Jima on February 23, 1945. National WWII Museum

The overall theme of this blog came from reading, Goodbye, Darkness: A Memoir of the Pacific War, by William Manchester. This was an excellent book about Manchester's time as a soldier during WWII. The story he tells truly shows the struggles American soldiers went through during WWII and how it not only affected them then, it left marks on them for the rest of their lives.

Pearl Harbor

Pearl Harbor was a devastating day for the United States, to quote Franklin D. Roosevelt, “it is a day that will live in infamy.”

Jungle Warfare and the Battle of Guadalcanal

After the war was declared on Japan by the United States, soldiers were shipped to Japan to fight. Japan had very dense jungles that US soldiers had to fight in during WWII.

The Economy, before, during, and after WWII

Not only did the US economy change very fast before, during, and after World War Two, the world economy also saw big changes.
